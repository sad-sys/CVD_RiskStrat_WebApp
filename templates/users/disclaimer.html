PRECISE-CVD — Important Notice

PRECISE-CVD is a research platform and is for research use only. It must not be used for clinical decision-making.

Do not use any output from this platform for:

  • Patient diagnosis
  • Treatment planning
  • Prescribing decisions
  • Any form of clinical care

The outputs of this platform do not constitute medical advice. Before making any healthcare decisions, please consult a qualified medical professional.

3. What personal data we collect

We may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Demographic information, including age, sex and ethnicity
  • Health data, including blood pressure, cholesterol, glucose, BMI, smoking status and medical history
  • Genetic data, including polygenic risk scores
  • Metabolomic data, including blood metabolite profiles
  • Questionnaire responses, including lifestyle, family history and psychosocial factors

4. How we collect your data

Data may be collected through:

  • Self-reported questionnaires completed through the platform
  • Electronic health records, where appropriate permissions are in place
  • Biological sample analysis, where applicable

5. Legal basis for processing

We process personal data under the following legal bases:

  • UK GDPR Article 6(1)(e) — Public Task: this research is conducted in the public interest and forms part of the University's official research functions.
  • UK GDPR Article 9(2)(j) — Scientific Research Purposes: health and other special-category data are processed for scientific research purposes in accordance with the Data Protection Act 2018, including Schedule 1, Part 1, Paragraph 4.

6. How we use your data

Your data may be used to:

  • Develop and validate cardiovascular risk-prediction models
  • Conduct research on relationships between biomarkers and disease outcomes
  • Improve population-scale screening programmes
  • Generate generalisable scientific knowledge

7. Data sharing

Your directly identifiable personal data will not be shared with third parties except where this has been appropriately authorised and protected.

Anonymised or aggregated data may be used in academic publications. Data may also be shared with approved research collaborators under appropriate data-protection agreements.

8. Data retention

Your data will be retained for the duration of the research project, which is currently expected to be five years. After the applicable retention period, the data will be anonymised, securely archived or securely deleted in accordance with King's College London policies and the study protocol.

9. Data security

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data. These measures may include encryption, access controls, separation of identifying information, restricted research-team access and secure storage on UK-based infrastructure.

10. Your rights under UK GDPR

Depending on the circumstances and the lawful basis used, you may have the following rights:

  • Right of access: you may request a copy of personal data held about you.
  • Right to rectification: you may request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: in certain circumstances, you may request deletion of your data. This right may be limited where deletion would seriously impair the research.
  • Right to restrict processing: you may request that processing be restricted in certain circumstances. This right may be limited in the research context.
  • Right to object: you may object to processing carried out under the public task lawful basis in certain circumstances.
  • Withdrawal from the study: you may stop participating at any time. Because the lawful basis for processing is public task rather than consent, withdrawal does not automatically require the deletion of information already collected. Requests will be assessed individually.
  • Right to data portability: this right may not apply, or may be limited, where processing is carried out as a public task or for scientific research.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: you may complain to King's College London or to the Information Commissioner's Office.

11. Automated decision-making

The platform uses algorithmic models to generate risk predictions. These predictions are produced solely for research purposes and are not used to make automated clinical decisions about individuals.

12. International transfers

Personal data is intended to be stored within the United Kingdom using Google Cloud Platform infrastructure located in UK data centres. We do not currently intend to transfer personal data outside the UK.

13. Changes to this notice

We may update this Privacy Notice when required. The latest version will be made available through this platform.

We will need to use information that you provide about yourself for this research project. This information may include your name, contact details and health information provided through the PRECISE-CVD platform.

Authorised people may use this information to conduct the research or to ensure that the research is being carried out correctly.

People who do not need to know your identity will not be able to see your name or contact details. Wherever possible, your research data will be identified using a study code instead.

How we keep your information secure

We will protect your information by:

  • Storing data on secure, password-protected University or approved research infrastructure
  • Restricting access to authorised members of the research team
  • Using a unique study code and keeping identifying information separate from research data
  • Encrypting data transfers and following University security policies
  • Training research staff in data protection and confidentiality

Your data is not intended to be shared outside the United Kingdom.

What happens after the study?

After the study finishes, we may retain some study data so that the results can be checked and the research can be verified.

Reports and publications will be written so that individuals cannot reasonably be identified. Study data will be retained for a maximum of five years after the study ends, unless a different period is required by law, the study protocol or University research-governance policies.

At the end of the retention period, data will be fully anonymised, securely archived or securely destroyed.

What choices do you have?

You may stop taking part in the study at any time without giving a reason. If you withdraw, we will not collect further information from you unless this is necessary to close your participation safely or meet a legal requirement.

Information already collected may continue to be retained and used where this is necessary to preserve the integrity of the research or meet legal and regulatory requirements.

You may ask us to remove, change or delete personal data held about you for the study. We may not always be able to fulfil the request where doing so would seriously impair the research. Where this applies, we will explain the reason.

Data collected through this study will not be used for unrelated future research without the appropriate ethical, legal and governance approvals.
